# Scoring Rubric — DomainForge Reference

Detailed evaluation criteria for each scoring factor. Total: 100 points.

---

## 1. Brandability (25 points)

The most important factor. A brandable name is ownable, distinctive, and evocative without being literal.

**25 pts:** Instantly evocative. Could be mistaken for a funded YC startup. Feels inevitable in retrospect. No one else would have this name.

**20 pts:** Strong brand potential. Distinctive, not generic. Minor issues (slight literal hint, or minor phonetic awkwardness).

**15 pts:** Decent. Works but forgettable. Might be used by other small companies in adjacent spaces.

**10 pts:** Generic. Describes the category. Could be anyone's product name.

**0-5 pts:** Terrible. Contains "Smart", "Pro", "Next", "Hub", "Platform", "Solution", "AI" as standalone suffix, or "Tech" as suffix.

**Questions to evaluate:**
- Would a journalist write this name in an article without needing to explain it?
- Could this appear on a billboard with only the name and a logo?
- Is it ownable in a crowded market?

---

## 2. Pronunciation (15 points)

The name must be sayable by anyone, anywhere. If someone can't say it on a podcast without spelling it out, it fails.

**15 pts:** Phonetically obvious. Pronounceable in English, Spanish, Mandarin, German with no ambiguity.

**12 pts:** Clear in English. Minor ambiguity in other languages (acceptable for US-focused products).

**8 pts:** Requires a moment of thought. Could be said two ways. Might cause embarrassment on a podcast.

**4 pts:** Regularly mispronounced. Confusing vowel combinations. Silent letters that aren't obvious.

**0 pts:** Unpronounceable or actively confusing.

**Red flags:**
- `gh` in unexpected positions
- `ph` that could be read as `f` or `p`
- Endings in `-cht`, `-gn`, `-mn`
- Double vowels with ambiguous pronunciation: `ei`, `ie`, `oe` mid-word

---

## 3. Memorability (15 points)

Will someone remember this name 48 hours after hearing it once?

**15 pts:** Sticks immediately. Phonetic hooks. Rhythm. Visual distinctiveness. Could be recalled after hearing in passing.

**12 pts:** Memorable with one repetition.

**8 pts:** Forgettable. Blends in with similar names in the space.

**4 pts:** Actively hard to remember. No hooks, no rhythm, no distinctiveness.

**0 pts:** Interchangeable with dozens of other names. No retrieval cue.

**What aids memorability:**
- Alliteration (partial or full)
- Unexpected consonant clusters
- Real words used out-of-context (Stripe is a stripe)
- Strong visual shape (short words with tall letters: k, l, h, t)
- Ends with a hard stop: Stripe, Slack, Brex, Spark

---

## 4. Length & Shape (10 points)

Shorter is almost always better. But more important than length is the visual and phonetic shape.

**10 pts:** 4-6 characters. Perfect. Works on favicon, app icon, business card, billboard.

**8 pts:** 7-8 characters. Good. Still works everywhere.

**6 pts:** 9-10 characters. Acceptable. Gets long in some contexts.

**3 pts:** 11-12 characters. Borderline. Will get truncated on mobile.

**0 pts:** 13+ characters, or contains hyphens, numbers, or special characters.

**Shape bonus (+2, applied outside rubric):** If the name has strong visual letter shape — ascenders and descenders creating visual rhythm — note it as a branding advantage. Example: `Figma` has visual balance. `Stripe` has strong uprights.

---

## 5. SEO Keyword Potential (10 points)

This factor's weight doubles (to 20) when the user is in SEO mode.

**10 pts:** Contains a high-volume keyword that is also not oversaturated. Domain doubles as a search entry point.

**7 pts:** Keyword adjacent. Topically relevant without being exact-match.

**4 pts:** No keyword value, but no SEO penalty either. Neutral.

**0 pts:** Exact-match domain for a spammy or oversaturated keyword, or name so generic it can't rank for its own brand term.

**Note:** For premium B2B SaaS and consumer brands, SEO weight is naturally lower. A name like "Linear" or "Notion" ranks for its brand, not keywords. Penalize this factor less for Unicorn and Premium modes.

---

## 6. Social Handle Availability (10 points)

Platforms checked: X/Twitter, GitHub, Instagram, LinkedIn.

**10 pts:** Handle available on all 4 major platforms.

**7 pts:** Available on 3/4. Missing one non-critical platform.

**4 pts:** Available on 2/4. X or GitHub taken is a significant issue for tech products.

**1 pt:** Only 1 available. Major friction for launch.

**0 pts:** Taken everywhere. Abandon this name unless it is exceptional in every other dimension.

**Platform priority by archetype:**
- DevTool: GitHub > X > Others
- Consumer app: Instagram > X > TikTok > Others
- B2B SaaS: LinkedIn > X > GitHub > Others
- Open source: GitHub > X > Others

---

## 7. Trademark Risk (10 points)

**10 pts:** No similar registered trademarks in any adjacent category. Clean search.

**7 pts:** Minor similarity to small/regional brands. Not an issue at launch, low risk.

**4 pts:** Similar to a known brand in a different industry. Use with caution. Legal opinion recommended before scaling.

**0 pts:** Clearly similar to a major brand, tech company, or recent acquisition target. Do not use.

**Auto-reject patterns:**
- Any name that sounds like: Google, Meta, Apple, Microsoft, Stripe, Vercel, Linear, OpenAI, Anthropic, Notion, Figma, GitHub, Nvidia, Adobe, Shopify, Twilio, Salesforce, HubSpot
- Names containing trademarked terms: GPT, Gemini, Copilot
- Names that could cause consumer confusion with a direct competitor

---

## 8. Viral Potential (5 points)

Can this name spread on its own? Would people use it as a verb or a reference?

**5 pts:** Has potential to become a verb or a cultural reference. ("Just Stripe it", "Loom me that")

**3 pts:** Natural to recommend. Easy to say in conversation. No friction in word-of-mouth.

**1 pt:** People would describe the product, not say the name. No natural referral hook.

**0 pts:** Actively awkward to say in conversation.

---

## Final Score Interpretation

| Score | Verdict |
|-------|---------|
| 90-100 | Exceptional. Present as #1 recommendation with enthusiasm. |
| 80-89 | Strong. Solid option, recommend confidently. |
| 70-79 | Good. Include in shortlist with notes on weaknesses. |
| 60-69 | Weak. Only show if nothing stronger is available. Add caveat. |
| Below 60 | Reject. Do not present to user. Iterate instead. |
