# Brand Archetypes — DomainForge Reference

Detailed naming conventions and real-world examples for each archetype.

---

## B2B SaaS

**Energy:** Clean, confident, metaphorical. The name implies movement, clarity, or structure without describing the feature.

**Naming rules:**
- 2 syllables preferred
- Avoid literal product descriptors
- Strong in .io, .com, .app
- Names should work on a pitch deck and a billboard equally

**Real examples:** Linear, Notion, Loom, Coda, Airtable, Asana, Monday, Intercom, Segment, Amplitude

**Generated in this style:** Florae, Tarka, Velta, Quell, Driftio, Relais, Lumora

---

## DevTool / CLI

**Energy:** Technical, fast, lowercase-first, terminal-native. The name should look good in a README code block.

**Naming rules:**
- Ultra short: 3-6 chars ideal
- Works in lowercase: `bun`, `zed`, `vite`, `elysia`, `hono`
- Hard consonants, one or two syllables
- .dev, .sh, .io, .build are natural TLDs

**Real examples:** Vite, Bun, Deno, Zed, Hono, Elysia, Nx, Turbo, Rspack, Biome

**Generated in this style:** Kolt, Trakt, Vex, Quik, Flox, Zarx, Pakt

---

## Consumer AI / Assistant

**Energy:** Warm, approachable, slightly magical. The name should make someone feel they're talking to a smart friend, not a machine.

**Naming rules:**
- Soft consonants: l, m, n, r, v
- Often ends in a vowel sound
- Works well with color-based brand identity
- .ai, .com, .app

**Real examples:** Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Otter, Arc, Luma, Cleo, Elsa

**Generated in this style:** Lumio, Vela, Miren, Norai, Elva, Cavio, Flori

---

## Fintech / Finance

**Energy:** Trust, precision, motion. The name should feel like a Bloomberg terminal meets a design studio.

**Naming rules:**
- Sharp, confident, no ambiguity
- Avoid anything that sounds like a bank from 1990
- Money metaphors are fine if subtle (Ramp, Brex)
- .com preferred for trust. .io acceptable for startup phase.

**Real examples:** Stripe, Brex, Ramp, Fey, Mercury, Woven, Pilot, Rippling

**Generated in this style:** Relo, Vault, Quota, Ledge, Pario, Finem, Strata

---

## Crypto / Web3

**Energy:** Abstract, futuristic, mythological, or mathematical. Conveys decentralization and boldness.

**Naming rules:**
- Abstract or mythological references work well
- Single strong word or invented word
- .xyz, .eth, .io, .network
- Avoid: "Chain", "Block", "Coin", "Token" as standalone words (oversaturated)

**Real examples:** Uniswap, Phantom, Zora, Blur, Magic Eden, Argent, Aztec, Arbitrum

**Generated in this style:** Zorex, Vanta, Epoch, Axiom, Phaos, Kairos, Ethra

---

## Marketplace / Community

**Energy:** Active verbs, community ownership, mutual benefit. The name should feel like a place people go, not a tool they use.

**Naming rules:**
- Often a noun that implies gathering or exchange
- Avoid corporate suffixes
- .com strongly preferred (trust for transactions)
- Names can be slightly longer (8-10 chars) if memorable

**Real examples:** Faire, Convoy, Lemon, Airbnb, Etsy, Depop, Whatnot

**Generated in this style:** Parcel, Agora, Cordo, Vaunt, Quorum, Relai, Bourse

---

## Agency / Studio / Creative

**Energy:** Distinctive, memorable, slightly mysterious. The name is itself a creative act.

**Naming rules:**
- Can be more unusual than product companies
- Single strong word or a word pair
- The name IS the brand — it doesn't need to hint at services
- .com, .studio, .co, .design

**Real examples:** Pentagram, Huge, Instrument, IDEO, Ueno, Frog, Wolff Olins, Character

**Generated in this style:** Sonder, Veld, Karst, Plinth, Tenon, Gravel, Serif

---

## Viral / Consumer App

**Energy:** Ultra-sticky, short enough to say in a conversation, works as a verb. "I'll just [name] it."

**Naming rules:**
- 4-6 characters max for elite tier
- Can be a real word used out of context (Snap, Zoom, Slack)
- Invented words must be phonetically obvious
- .com ideally, .app acceptable

**Real examples:** Snap, Zoom, Slack, Loom, Figma, Duolingo, Vine, Kik, Byte

**Generated in this style:** Flick, Noti, Velo, Quip, Swiv, Drft, Glim

---

## Open Source / Indie Hacker

**Energy:** Clever, slightly nerdy, expressive. The community should feel like they discovered a secret.

**Naming rules:**
- Wordplay and references welcome
- Can be slightly longer if the reference is clever
- Works in `.dev`, `.sh`, `.tools`, `.fyi`
- GitHub org name matters as much as domain

**Real examples:** Excalidraw, Mermaid, Obsidian, Logseq, Foam, Tana, Anytype

**Generated in this style:** Dendron, Vellum, Glyph, Praxis, Quartz, Ligature, Motif
